## 3. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the main difference between Backup and Disaster Recovery?**  
A: Cloud Backup is designed for long-term data retention and "point-in-time" file recovery. Disaster Recovery (DR) is for **immediate infrastructure continuity**, allowing you to switch your entire server operation to a standby site within a minute of a failure.

**Q: What are RPO and RTO in the context of GreenNode DR?**  
A: **RPO (Recovery Point Objective)** is 15 minutes, meaning the maximum data loss window is only 15 minutes. **RTO (Recovery Time Objective)** is under 1 minute, meaning your systems can be back online almost instantly.

**Q: How does the "Failover" process work?**  
A: When your primary site experiences an issue, you initiate a "Failover" through the GreenNode console. The system automatically points your operations to the synchronized standby server, restoring service availability immediately.

**Q: Can I restore to a specific point in time?**  
A: Yes. We store up to 30 historical copies over a 30-day period, giving you the flexibility to recover from logical errors (like data corruption or accidental deletion) by rolling back to a clean state.

**Q: Is the DR environment always running?**  
A: The data replication is continuous, but the backup compute resources are only fully activated during a failover or a planned test, helping you optimize costs while maintaining readiness.
